What is PartPercent% of Whole?

PartPercent * Multiple

Having PartPercent% of something means that you get PartPercent out of every 100.

We can set up a proportion to find out what number is PartPercent% of Whole:

\qquad \dfrac{\color{BLUE}{\text{percent}}}{100} = \dfrac{\color{PINK}{\text{part}}}{\color{GREEN}{\text{whole}}}

Which things do we know, and what are we trying to find?

We know the \color{BLUE}{\text{percent}} is PartPercent. Is Whole the \color{PINK}{\text{part}} or the \color{GREEN}{\text{whole}}?

The Whole is the \color{GREEN}{\text{whole}}. We are trying to find the \color{PINK}{\text{part}} that makes up PartPercent% of it:

\qquad \dfrac{\color{BLUE}{PartPercent}}{100} = \dfrac{\color{PINK}{\text{part}}}{\color{GREEN}{Whole}}

If we multiply the denominator of the fraction on the left by Multiple, it will be the same denominator of the fraction on the right. To keep things equal, let's also multiply the numerator on the left by Multiple:

\qquad \dfrac{\color{BLUE}{PartPercent} \times Multiple}{100 \times Multiple} = \dfrac{\color{PINK}{\text{part}}}{\color{GREEN}{Whole}}

\qquad \dfrac{\color{BLUE}{PartPercent * Multiple}}{Whole} = \dfrac{\color{PINK}{\text{part}}}{\color{GREEN}{Whole}}

\qquad \color{BLUE}{PartPercent * Multiple} = \color{PINK}{\text{part}}

So PartPercent * Multiple is PartPercent% of Whole.
